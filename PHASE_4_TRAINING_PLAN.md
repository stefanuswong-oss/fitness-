## What the data actually shows

I read all 6 Phase 3 program PDFs and cross-checked them against your logged sessions (June 29 – July 23).

**The programs, as written, already contain the redundancy — you're following them correctly, the design is the problem.**

1. **Chest/shoulders are trained 3x/week, not 2x.** Monday (Push Strength) and Thursday (Push Hypertrophy) are your two intended push days. But Saturday's "Legs + Core / Full Body / Hyrox" quietly turned into a *third* full chest-and-shoulder session in Phase 3 weeks 2–3 ("Full Body (no back)": shoulder press, lateral raise, rear delt fly, chest press, pec fly, decline press — that's a complete push workout bolted onto a day meant for legs and conditioning). That's the single biggest driver of both your 2-hour sessions and, likely, your stalled visual progress — you're accumulating fatigue on the same muscles three times a week without the frequency doing anything extra for growth once you're already covering it twice.

2. **Each session stacks too many exercises for the same movement pattern.** Example: your June 29 Push Strength session had DB Incline Press, Iso-leverage decline chest press, and Machine Chest Press — three different heavy pressing variations in one sitting, plus a fly, plus a shoulder press, plus a 4-round lateral raise drop-set ladder (effectively ~20 mini-sets for side delts alone), plus three separate triceps exercises. Your Pull days do the same thing with 4–5 different lat-pulldown/row variations back to back. This is classic junk volume: past a certain point, more sets for the same muscle in one session mostly buys you time and fatigue, not more stimulus.

3. **Drop-set ladders are a big, invisible time cost.** Several of your lateral raise and pressing sets are logged as long strings like "1 x 12,12,12,12,15,15,15, 1 x 10,12,12,12,15,15,15..." — that's not 3 sets, it's closer to 20+ mini-sets in one exercise slot. These add up to 15–20 minutes on their own.

4. **Abs are trained at the end of nearly every single session** (Push, Pull, Legs, Saturday) — low cost individually, but it's volume you don't need daily and it's part of why sessions run long.

None of this means your training is "bad" — the exercise selection is sound and your loads have progressed steadily (e.g., V-Squat 90→110kg, Machine Lat Pulldown 50→70kg over Phase 3). It means the program has scope creep: more exercises and sets got added over time than your recovery or your schedule can support.

## On "abs-ready by Oct 17"

Your last body scan (2026-07-04) had you at 15% body fat, 68.6kg. Visible abs for most people show up somewhere around 10–12% body fat. Closing that gap in ~12 weeks is achievable but it is overwhelmingly a **nutrition/caloric deficit** problem, not a training-volume problem — no amount of extra lifting reveals abs without the fat loss. The trimmed program below is built to preserve/build muscle and free up time for conditioning, but the deficit has to come from your diet. Worth tracking body scans monthly through October to make sure you're trending down, not just recomping in place.

## On keeping chest/shoulders at 3x/week (added 2026-07-24)

Fair pushback: frequency itself isn't the problem — total weekly volume and per-session redundancy is. Training a muscle 3x/week is a legitimate, commonly used approach and can out-perform 2x/week *if* weekly sets are distributed sensibly across the three sessions rather than stacked on top of each other at full volume each time.

The original Saturday session was a problem specifically because it duplicated Monday/Thursday almost set-for-set (full shoulder press, lateral raise, rear delt fly, chest press, pec fly, decline press) — that's not "3x/week frequency," that's 3 full sessions' worth of volume in a week, which is where junk volume and recovery debt creep in.

The fix: keep the third touch, but make it small. With Monday (~7 chest sets, ~6 shoulder-press sets, ~3 lateral sets) and Thursday (~6 chest, ~3 shoulder-press, ~3 lateral) already covering the bulk of the work, Saturday only needs 2 exercises at 2 sets each, kept a few reps short of failure, to add a frequency stimulus without adding meaningful fatigue. That lands weekly totals around **~15 chest sets and ~14 combined shoulder-press + lateral sets** — solidly inside the normal 10-20 sets/week hypertrophy range for an intermediate lifter, instead of the 25-30+ sets/week the original 3-full-session version was likely producing.

Rule going forward for Saturday's push work specifically: light weight, stop well short of failure, no drop sets. If it starts creeping back toward a "real" session (heavier weight, closer to failure, more sets), you're back to the original problem.

## Why this should help, mechanically

- Push and pull muscle groups go from 3x/week to a clean 2x/week (Mon/Thu, Tue/Fri) — enough frequency for growth, without the added fatigue that wasn't producing extra results.
- Each session drops from 9-12 exercises to 5-6, and drop-set ladders are capped at one per session — this alone should cut 30-45 minutes off session length.
- Saturday actually becomes glutes/core/conditioning again, which supports the fat-loss side of "abs by Oct 17" instead of adding more upper-body fatigue.
- Legs Strength (Wednesday) was already efficient and is left mostly untouched.

Track it for 3-4 weeks and re-check the body scan — if load is progressing on the main lifts and the scan shows fat trending down, this is working. If not, that's the point to loop back in on nutrition and/or the Ipamorelin question with your doctor.
